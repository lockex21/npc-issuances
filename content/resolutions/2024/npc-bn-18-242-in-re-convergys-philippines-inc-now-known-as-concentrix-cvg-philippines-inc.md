RESOLUTION

NAGA , P.C.;

Before the Commission is the Compliance submitted by Convergys

Philippines, Inc. (Convergys) dated 26 March 2021.

Facts

On 28 December 2018, Convergys notified the National Privacy

Commission (NPC) through a letter regarding a breach incident

involving the medical condition of its employee, MAD. 1 In its report,

Convergys stated that on 27 December 2018, the Human Resources

(HR) team of its Clark Site located at SM City Clark IT Park, Angeles

City, Pampanga received an email from MA D reporting a possible

privacy violation of his immediate supervisor, DA .2

Further, MA D stated that he sent to DA a copy of his medical certificate

which indicated his illness and asked him to keep the matter between

themselves. 3 However, DA shared the medical certificate of MA D to

the other managers and noted his illness in the e -Start system of the

company which serves as an attendance tool to which other operations

supervisors and managers have access. 4

On 11 March 2021, the NPC ’s Complaints and Investigation Division

(CID) issued an Order directing Convergys to submit a Post -Breach

Report. 15

On 26 March 2021, Convergys submitted its Compliance to the Order

root cause of the incident was due to human error committed by DA

when the medical certificate was uploaded in the e-Start system .17

Convergys explained that employees are required to submit medical

certificates to their supervisors to support their absences. 18

“The root cause is the inadvertent human error committed by DA.

Employees submit their medical certificates (which may or may

not contain an illness) to their respective supervisors to support

their absences. These medical certificates are kept confidential, are

shared to the second level manager to get an approval to excuse

an absence by an employee. It is not standard however, to put in

the attendance tool the actual contents of the medical certi ficate

(and illness, if any) of an emplovee. DA inadverten tly captured

the medical condition of MA D in the attendance tool.” 19

Further, Convergys stated that DA was called out for his negligent act

in the incident. 20  DA together with the second level manager “were

directed to maintain confidentiality of the medical condition of

MA D."21  Thereafter, Convergys alleged that its HR team immediately

deleted the medical certificate of MA D in the attendance tool. 22

Accordingly, MA D was notified regarding the incident through

email .23  A proof and copy of the data subject notification was attached

Issue

Whether or not Convergys has sufficiently addressed the breach

incident pursuant to NPC Circular 16 -03 (Personal Data Breach

Management) .

Discussion

The Commission holds that Convergys has sufficiently addressed the

breach incident pursuant to NPC Circular 16 -03 (Personal Data Breach

Management) . As such, the Commission resolves to terminate the

investigation and consider the matter closed .

Although the Commission finds that the incident falls under

mandatory data subject notification, Convergys has already notified

the affected data subject and implemented measures to prevent the

recurrence of the incident.

Section 1 1 of NPC Circular 16 -03 provides:

SECTION 11. When notification is required.

Notification shall be required upon knowledge of or when there

is reasonable belief by the personal information controller or

personal information processor that a personal data breach

requiring notification has occurred, under the following

conditions:

A. The personal data involves sensitive personal information

or any other information that may be used to enable identity

fraud. For this purpose, “other information” shall include, but

not be limited to: data about the financial or economic situation

of the data subject; usernames, passwords and other login data;

biometric data; copies of identification documents, licenses or

unique identifiers like Philhealth, SSS, GSIS, TIN number; or

other similar information, which m ay be made the basis of

decisions concerning the data subject, including the grant of

rights or benefits.

B. There is reason to believe that the information may have been

acquired by an unauthorized person ; and

C. The personal information controller or the Commission

believes that the unauthorized acquisition is likely to give rise

to a real risk of serious harm to any affected data subject.

(emphasis supplied) 30

condition of MA D is sensitive personal information as contemplated

Circular, the following terms are defined, as follows:

“Sensitive personal information” refers to personal information:

1. About an individual’s race, ethnic origin, marital status, age,

color, and religious, philosophical or political affiliations;

2. About an individual’s health , education, genetic or sexual life

of a person, or to any proceeding for any offense committed or

alleged to have been committed by such person, the disposal of

Regarding the second element , there is reason to believe that the

information was acquired by an unauthorized person because the

other supervisors were able to access the medical certificate of MA D.

Further, Convergys admitted that other managers had access to the e-

Start system. Thus, it must be emphasized , that these supervisors were

never privy to the said document. In this regard , the access of these

unauthorized individuals to the medical record of MA D would likely

give rise to a real risk of serious harm when combined with MA D’s

personal information and used in a manner that could expose him to

harassment or discrimination . Thus, the third element is also present.

Following Section 11 of NPC Circular 16 -03, t he Commission finds that

the incident falls under mandatory notification considering that there

is sensitive personal information involved, it was acquired by an

unauthorized person, and such acquisition is likely to give rise to a real

risk of serious harm.

Nonetheless, it bears stressing that Convergys has sufficiently notified

the affected data subject. Section 18 (C) of NPC Circular 16 -03 provides

procedures:

be limited to:

1. nature of the breach;

2. personal data possibly involved;

Moreover, Convergys issued directives to the supervisors involved to

keep the confidentiality of the information. 34  Finally, Convergys has

provided the contact details of its data privacy team whom MA D may

contact if there would be untoward incidents that would transpire as

a result of the incident. 35

As to the notification to the Commission , Convergys sent the same on

28 December 2018, which is within seventy -two (72) hours from the ir

knowledge of the incident on 27 December 2018 .36  In its submission ,

the nature of the breach, the personal data involved, and the measures

taken to address the breach were reported. 37  Thus, Convergys has

sufficiently complied with the reportorial requirement s under Section

17 of NPC Circular 16 -03 .

Considering the foregoing, the Commission finds that Convergys has

already complied with its directives and NPC Circular 16 -03 (Personal

Data Breach Management) . Consequently, the Commission deems it

proper to close and terminate the case.

WHEREFORE , premises considered, this Commission resolves that

the matter of NPC BN 18 -142 “In re: Convergys Philippines, Inc. (now

known as Concentrix CVG Philippines, Inc.)” is hereby considered

CLOSED .
