Commission. 3

attack on several websites of both government and private entities. 4

websites. 5 Among the entities affected by this attack was DepEd

Issue

Whether DepEd Calamba has sufficiently complied with the Order

dated 15 July 2022 , including the implementation of reasonable and

appropriate security measures .

Discussion

The Commission finds that DepEd Calamba has sufficiently complied

with the Order dated 15 July 2022 since the affected data subjects were

sufficiently notified in accordance with NPC Circular 16 -03 , and that

DepEd Calamba has implemented reasonable and appropriate

security measures to address the breach and prevent a recurrence of

the incident . As such, the Commission resolves to consider the matter

closed.

I.  DepEd Calamba sufficiently

notified the data subjects who

were affected by the breach .

DepEd Calamba notified the affected data subject s following Rule V,

Section s 18 (C) and (D) of NPC Circular 16 -03 :

SECTION 18. Notification of Data Subjects .

affected by a personal data breach, subject to the following

procedures:

2.  personal data possibly involved;

consequences of the breach;

information systems which includes the Division of Calamba

remove the probability of using the employee number in loan

In addition, you are hereby required to attend an orientation and

risk assessment meeting on April 26, 2018, 1:30 pm at the

harm that this incident may cause.

information, Section 20 (a) of the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (DPA)

provides: 41

Sec. 20. Security of Personal Information.

a. The personal information controller must implement

reasonable and appropriate organizational, physical, and

technical measures intended for the protection of personal

information against any accidental or unlawful destruction,

alteration and disclosure, as well as against any other unlawful

processing.

• Regular password reset

Information Systems

• All information systems were migrated from online to

local network implementation

Finally, DepEd Calamba also stated that it will implement security

All told, t he implementation of the abovementioned measures after the

data breach , as well as the notification made by DepEd Calamba on its

own initiative, demonstrated its diligence and commitment to protect

the rights of its data subjects and abide by the DPA and the other

issuances of the NPC . DepEd Calamba was able to mitigate the risks

caused by the incident through its existing security measures and the

notification it sent to the affected data subjects.

In view of the foregoing, the Commission holds that DepEd Calamba

ha s sufficiently notified the affected data subjects, and it has

implemented reasonable and appropriate security measures to

address the breach and prevent a recurrence of the incident.

WHEREFORE, premises considered, the Commission resolves that the

matter of NPC BN 18 -054 “In re : Department of Educatio n-Calamba ”

is  hereby CLOSED .
