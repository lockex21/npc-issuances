                                                 ADVISORY
                                ON ONLINE LENDING PLATFORMS

TO THE PUBLIC:

The government recently received numerous reports of online lending platforms (OLPs)
engaging in harassment, intimidation, public shaming, and unlawful use of personal data
in their collection practices.

Pursuant to the directive of the President to strengthen consumer protection in the digital
space and uphold the dignity and safety of every Filipino, the Department of Information
and Communications Technology (DICT), National Privacy Commission (NPC), and
Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) reiterate the provisions of existing issuances1
on the processing of personal data for loan-related transactions, specifically for lending
companies (LCs), financing companies (FCs), and other entities offering or facilitating
loans through OLPs2, whether recorded or unrecorded:

•   Unnecessary processing of personal data through mobile applications, including
    requiring unnecessary permissions, is prohibited.

•   Unauthorized, excessive, or disproportionate processing of personal data, particularly
    regarding access to borrowers’ contact lists, is prohibited. This refers to processing that
    is unconstrained, excessive, and disproportional to its purpose, such as, but is not
    limited to:
        a. Processing that leads to harassment;
        b. Processing for the collection of debt outside of the guarantors provided by the
            borrower; and
        c. Processing that results in unfair collection practices: those which use or involve
            threats of violence or other criminal means to harm the physical person,
            reputation or property of any person, as well as those which use threats to take
            any action that cannot be legally taken.

•   Contacting persons on the borrower's contact list other than those named as
    guarantors is prohibited. For purposes of debt collection, LCs, FCs, or persons acting
    as such may only contact the guarantor.

•   OLPs must have separate interfaces where data subjects can provide: (1) character
    references who are provided solely for identification or verification purposes; and (2)
    guarantors, who have expressly consented to assume the responsibility of the loan in
    case of default and who may be contacted in relation to loan obligations. To be
    considered a guarantor, one must have given consent to be a guarantor.

•   LCs, FCs, and other persons acting as such shall comply with the applicable NPC
    Rules on Registration of Data Processing Systems and Notifications regarding
    Automated Decision-Making.

•   LCs, FCs, and other persons acting as such shall retain personal data of borrowers only
    for as long as necessary for the fulfillment of the purposes for which the data was
    obtained, for the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims, or as provided by
    law. Thereafter, they shall ensure secure and proper disposal and destruction of
    personal data that would render further processing impossible.

•   When the purpose for an application permission has already been achieved, OLPs
    shall prompt the data subject to turn off, disallow, or revoke such permission.

Individuals availing of loans through OLPs are likewise reminded to be vigilant in
protecting their personal data through the following actions:

•   Download OLPs from official or verified sources only and ensure that such OLPs are
    operated by duly registered and licensed entities.

•   Read privacy notices and consent forms carefully. Some OLPs might use deceptive
    design patterns or design techniques that aim to manipulate or deceive data subjects
    to perform a specific act relating to the processing of their personal data, such as:
        a. pre-ticked boxes for granting permissions;
        b. interface that makes giving consent easy but makes withdrawing consent
           difficult;
        c. prominently highlighted choices that results in greater personal data
           processing, whereas the alternative that supports data minimization is
           obscured.

    The use of deceptive design patterns undermines the general data privacy principles
    and the rights of data subjects and may invalidate consent.

•   Review the permissions being required by OLPs: these must not request unnecessary
    permissions unless needed for specified and legitimate purposes, e.g., access to your
    camera or photo gallery is permitted only for identity verification, know-your-
    customer (KYC), or similar specified and legitimate purposes, and must be turned off
    once the purpose has been fulfilled.

•   OLPs may only access your contact list to allow you to select character references,
    guarantors, or to derive proportional metadata when necessary for specified and
    legitimate purposes. Unbridled processing of contact lists is prohibited.

    authority to operate, and other penalties provided under relevant laws.
