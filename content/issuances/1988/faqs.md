## Issuance Text

**Circular No. 20-02**

### Frequently Asked Questions on the Draft Rules on the Issuance of Cease and Desist Orders (CDO)

1. **Does Section 4(B) in laying one of the requisites for the issuance of a CDO as "such act or practice is detrimental to national security or public interest, or the CDO is necessary to preserve and protect the rights of a data subject," expand [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012#section-7-functions-of-the-national-privacy-commission|Section 7(C) of the Data Privacy Act]]?** No. The phrase is taken from Section 9(f)(3) of the [[laws/implementing-rules-and-regulations-of-the-data-privacy-act-of-2012|IRR]] of the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|DPA]], which gives the NPC enforcement power to issue cease and desist orders or impose a temporary or permanent ban when processing is detrimental to national security or public interest, or when necessary to preserve and protect the rights of data subjects. The [[laws/implementing-rules-and-regulations-of-the-data-privacy-act-of-2012|IRR]] did not expand the DPA; it contextualized the public-interest ground within the NPC's jurisdiction. Before a CDO may be issued, the applicant must still prove that the issue falls under national security or public interest and that it relates to the preservation and protection of data subject rights.

2. **The CDO is issued ex parte. Is this not a violation of the due process rule?** No. The CDO is an extraordinary remedy reserved for cases where the commission or continuance of an act or practice, unless restrained, will cause grave and irreparable injury to a data subject ([[issuances/undated/rules-on-the-issuance-of-cease-and-desist-orders|CDO Rules]], Section 4(C)). Due process is satisfied by the order requiring the adverse party to comment on the issued CDO, which is then set for adjudication by the Commission En Banc. After the respondent is heard, the Commission decides whether to extend or lift the CDO no later than thirty (30) days from the expiration of the period to comment or the termination of any clarificatory hearing. If the Commission does not decide within that period, the CDO is automatically lifted.

3. **How can the NPC justify an ex parte CDO when the DPA does not authorize the NPC to issue the same?** Jurisprudence recognizes the implied power of quasi-judicial agencies to issue ex parte cease and desist orders in accordance with their mandate. An administrative agency has not only the powers expressly granted by law, but also those necessarily implied in the exercise of its express powers; otherwise, it may be reduced to a "toothless" paper agency (Laguna Lake Development Authority vs Court of Appeals, GR 110120, March 16, 1994).

4. **Can you further qualify "grave and irreparable injury" or set the criteria for said injury?** Damages are irreparable when there is no standard by which their amount can be measured with reasonable accuracy. An irreparable injury includes repeated or continuing wrongs that produce hurt, inconvenience, or damage that can be estimated only by conjecture, and injuries that cannot be fairly recompensed by pecuniary value (Power Sites and Signs Inc. vs. United Neon, etc., G.R. 163406, November 24, 2009).

5. **Is this similar to a preliminary injunction under Rule 58 of the Rules of Civil Procedure?** Yes. The purpose of a CDO and a preliminary injunction is the same. Under Section 5 of Rule 58 of the Rules of Court, an injunction may issue ex parte when a verified application shows that grave and irreparable injury would result before the matter is heard on notice.

6. **What is the difference between a CDO and a temporary or permanent ban on the processing of personal information?** The CDO is an independent action that covers the processing of personal information and any act or practice in violation of the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|DPA]]. It is commenced motu propio by the Complaints and Investigation Division (CID) or the Compliance and Monitoring Division (CMD), or through a verified application by the aggrieved party upon recommendation by the CID. It is issued ex parte after verification and investigation, and remains in force until lifted or modified by the Commission. A temporary or permanent ban, on the other hand, is a provisional remedy that only covers processing of personal information. It is commenced upon complaint or before finality of a Commission decision, issued after summary hearing and notice, and remains in effect until final resolution or further lawful order ([[issuances/undated/personal-data-breach-management|NPC Circular 16-03]]).

7. **From a practical perspective, is there still a benefit to seeking a temporary or permanent ban on processing in relation to a complaint instead of applying for a CDO?** There can be, but the remedies address different grounds. A party cannot apply for a CDO if only the grounds for a temporary or permanent ban are present.

8. **Should this not be merely an accessory to an original action?** No. The application for a CDO may or may not be attached to a complaint. An aggrieved party may file an application for a CDO without a complaint when the desired remedy is the issuance of the CDO against the adverse party.

9. **Section 5 of the CDO Rules provide "filing with the Commission of an application in writing." Is there a specific office where this can be filed or will it be filed with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner and the two Deputy Privacy Commissioners?** The application is filed with the "Commission," composed of the Privacy Commissioner and the two Deputy Privacy Commissioners, through the NPC's General Record Unit. The CID first assesses whether the application is sufficient in form and substance before transmitting it, with its recommendation, to the Commission, which ultimately decides on the propriety of the application and the necessity of issuing the CDO.

10. **What is the standard of proof in the issuance of the CDO?** Substantial evidence is the standard of proof in the issuance of a CDO, as it is the standard used for final decisions of quasi-judicial agencies. Substantial evidence is such relevant evidence that a reasonable mind might accept as adequate to support a conclusion, and complainants in administrative proceedings bear the burden of proving their allegations with that standard (De Jesus v. Guerrero III, G.R. No. 171491, September 4, 2019; Office of the Ombudsman v. Loving Fetalvero Jr., G.R. No. 211450, July 23, 2018).

11. **How will the bond be computed? How can CID assess or estimate any potential damage that the PIC/Adverse Party may suffer due to malicious or erroneously issued CDO? What will be their basis?** The bond will be computed based on the assessment of the CID. Further details, including the rules on assessment, the period for posting the bond, and the effect of non-posting, will be covered by another circular on fines, fees, and penalties.

12. **Is it possible for the adverse party to file a counterbond?** No. A counterbond discharges the writ of attachment enforced against the respondent, but a CDO is an extraordinary remedy reserved for cases involving national security and public interest where continued conduct would cause grave and irreparable injury. Allowing a counterbond would go against that policy, and the nature of the harm involved is incapable of pecuniary estimation.

13. **What is the rule on filing and service?** Filing and service follow [[issuances/undated/rules-of-procedure|NPC Circular 16-04]] or the Rules of Procedure of the NPC as amended. Filing may be made personally or electronically, while service may be made by personal or substituted service; if those are impossible, service may be made by private courier.

14. **Explain the rationale behind the phrase "The lifting of the CDO shall not preclude the issuance of another CDO based on the same acts complained of, should such acts after lifting of the CDO, would then continue within twelve (12) months from its lifting."** The phrase means the Commission may issue another CDO only upon verification and investigation, without a new application. It is intended to provide an immediate remedy when a respondent stops the complained-of acts to lift the CDO and then resumes them shortly thereafter.

15. **What happens if there is a CDO that refers to the same processing lodged as a complaint, breach notification, or compliance check, and the CID or CMD process is not yet done but the CDO has been executed already?** If there is a pending complaint or investigation under CID, or a compliance check or breach notification pending with the CMD, and there is also an application for a CDO on the same matter, both may proceed independently. They are separate proceedings.

16. **Will a CDO be proper after a mere privacy sweep or document submission? Will a Data Breach Notification by itself be a good cause for the issuance of a CDO?** Each application is decided case by case after verification and investigation. A CDO may issue after or during a compliance check or breach notification if the Commission finds it proper under the circumstances.

17. **At which stage will the Complaints and Investigations Division (CID) recommend the issuance of a CDO? Is it possible the CID will recommend a CDO even before the order to confer for discovery?** The CID will not recommend issuance of a CDO. Once an application is filed, the CID only makes a preliminary assessment to determine whether the application is sufficient in form and substance; the Commission ultimately decides whether to grant the CDO.

18. **What is the remedy if the CID does not recommend the filing of the application?** The CID recommends only whether the application is sufficient in form and substance. It then forwards the application to the Commission together with its recommendation, and the Commission decides on the propriety of the application and the necessity of issuing a CDO. If the application is denied, the remedy lies against the Commission's decision.

19. **Does the rule on exhaustion of administrative remedies apply in this case?** No. Because an application for a CDO may proceed independently of a complaint, Section 4 of [[issuances/undated/rules-of-procedure|NPC Circular 16-04]] does not apply. The CDO is an extraordinary remedy intended to give immediate relief when the stringent statutory criteria are met, and requiring exhaustion would defeat that purpose.

20. **What will be the basis of the CID for conducting a sua sponte investigation?** The NPC may initiate an investigation of the circumstances surrounding a possible data privacy violation or personal data breach in cases arising from pending cases before the NPC, reports from the daily news, trends or academic studies, information from corroborated and substantiated anonymous tips, or reports from other offices of the Commission.

21. **The CDO also talks about "future action," or "threatening to do something." In this case, how will this fall under the jurisdiction of the NPC if there is no violation, to begin with?** The CDO is an extraordinary remedy intended to prevent grave and irreparable injury. Future actions, by implication, are covered by the Rules when they would cause such injury if not prevented.

22. **Can a CDO be immediately published regardless of the public interest or public education?** Publication follows [[issuances/2020/protocols-for-the-publication-of-decisions-resolutions-and-orders-on-the-npc-website|NPC Advisory 2020-01]]. That advisory generally limits publication of certain decisions, but in the case of a CDO, publication occurs only after the adverse party is heard and only when the Commission determines that public interest warrants it.

23. **Does the "Aggrieved Party" include a juridical person? May the corporation file a petition for issuance of CDO on behalf of its employees?** No. An aggrieved party or data subject as contemplated by the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|DPA]] refers to a natural person because privacy is a fundamental human right. A natural person may, however, be represented by a juridical person under [[issuances/undated/rules-of-procedure|NPC Circular 16-04]].

24. **May the aggrieved party be represented by counsel?** Yes, based on the suppletory application of the Rules of Court as well as [[issuances/undated/rules-of-procedure|NPC Circular 16-04]].

25. **Does this apply to online sellers, who due to the quarantine restrictions are maximizing mobile banking and online transactions?** Yes, these Rules apply to every personal information controller or processor.

26. **How will the penalties or fines be imposed? Will there a table of penalties be included in this circular or referred to?** The penalties, fees, fines, and the determination of the bond will be subject to a separate circular.
