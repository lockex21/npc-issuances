## Issuance Text

**Circular No. 2022**

**SECTION 1.** ***Objective.*** This Circular expands [[issuances/undated/guidelines-on-the-processing-of-personal-data-for-loan-related-transactions|NPC Circular No. 20-01]] to respond to emerging exigencies in processing personal data for loan-related transactions by lending and financing companies and similar actors.

**SECTION 2.** ***Amendments.*** The following provisions of [[issuances/undated/guidelines-on-the-processing-of-personal-data-for-loan-related-transactions|NPC Circular No. 20-01]] are hereby amended as described below:

   A. Section 3(A) is expanded with two new paragraphs:

   5. LCs, FCs, and similar actors shall obtain consent when the requested personal data is necessary, providing just-in-time notices immediately before collecting the data. A just-in-time notice explains how the specific information will be processed and is delivered when the entity is about to process the relevant personal data.

   6. Details about processing, mandated by Section 16(b) of the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|DPA]] and Section 34(a)(2) of the [[laws/implementing-rules-and-regulations-of-the-data-privacy-act-of-2012|IRR]], should follow whichever format aligns with the entity’s business process while remaining accessible and convenient for borrowers. For mobile applications, the relevant information must be readily accessible in-app.

   B. Section 3(D) is rewritten with clearer subsections and bullet text:

   D. Online loan applications may not perform unnecessary processing or require unnecessary personal data permissions.

   1. Mobile applications should request access to personal data only when the permission is suitable, necessary, and not excessive for the legitimate purposes listed in Section 3(B)(1) and Section 3(C), as well as debt collection, consistent with applicable law. Processing of permission-based data, such as contacts or cameras, may begin only when needed for those purposes, and any non-permission-collected data must still be processed proportionally.
        2. Once the purpose of an application permission is achieved and no additional lawful criterion applies, the application must prompt or allow the borrower to revoke or turn off that permission.
        3. When a mobile application accesses the borrower’s camera or gallery for KYC, fraud prevention, payment verification, or similar legitimate purposes, that access may be permitted during that stage but must be turned off afterward; borrowers should be notified that the permission can be revoked. Once the photo is captured and stored, the related permission should default to off and borrowers should receive a just-in-time reminder that it can be disabled. Collected photos must never be used to harass borrowers during collection.
        4. Processing contact lists may be allowed only to derive proportional metadata[^1] when it satisfies Section 3(D)(1) and Section 4 requirements. “Contact list” includes phone, email, or social media lists. Unbridled processing, meaning unconstrained, excessive, or disproportional activities such as harassment, contacting guarantors beyond those provided by the borrower, or unfair collection practices, is expressly prohibited.[^2]
        5. Subject to the preceding limitations, contact list processing is permitted to identify and contact character references or guarantors the borrower provides. Online lending applications must offer a dedicated interface where borrowers can deliberately choose such references from their contact lists, and LC/FC entities may only access that data to the extent necessary for selection.

   C. Section 3 gains the following subsections:

   G. As part of NPC registration, LCs, FCs, and similar entities must supply a complete list of publicly available applications they own or operate, including those supporting loan processing, in line with the Rules on Registration of Data Processing Systems and Notifications regarding Automated Decision-Making.

   H. PIPs and third-party service providers operating in the Philippines under the instruction of LCs, FCs, or similar parties must also register with the NPC when processing personal data.

   I. For overseas PIPs, the LC/FC must ensure appropriate technical and contractual safeguards that respect Sections 28–29 and 43–45 of the [[laws/implementing-rules-and-regulations-of-the-data-privacy-act-of-2012|IRR of the DPA]].

   J. Upon determining a violation of this Circular, the NPC may revoke the PIC’s or PIP’s registration after notice and an opportunity to explain, according to existing revocation rules.

   K. Entities whose registration is revoked or who are found in violation of registration requirements are subject to penalties and disciplinary measures under the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|DPA]], its [[laws/implementing-rules-and-regulations-of-the-data-privacy-act-of-2012|IRR]], and other NPC issuances.

   D. Section 4 now reads:

   **SECTION 4.** ***Character references.*** A character reference is someone whose contact information attests to the borrower’s identity and the accuracy of the supplied details.

   A. Borrowers may need to furnish names and contact numbers of character references, and must inform those references of their inclusion.
        B. LCs, FCs, and other persons acting as such shall adopt policies governing the handling of character references’ personal data, including guidance on call handling.
        C. Entities must inform character references how they were chosen and offer an option to remove their data as a reference.
        D. Contacting character references for unauthorized purposes—including marketing, cross-selling, or sharing their data with third parties—is prohibited.
        E. A character reference is not, by default, a guarantor.

   E. Section 5 is restated as follows:

   **SECTION 5.** ***Guarantors.*** A guarantor expressly agrees to satisfy the borrower’s obligation if the borrower fails to do so, subject to the Civil Code guaranty provisions.

   A. The guarantor’s separate consent must be obtained by the LC, FC, or other person acting as such, consistent with the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|DPA]] transparency and consent requirements for lawful processing.
        B. For debt collection, only the named guarantor may be contacted; contacting other individuals from the borrower’s contact list violates this Circular and the Securities and Exchange Commission’s guidance on unfair debt collection practices.[^3]

   F. The succeeding Sections on Credit Data, Outsourcing, Rights of the data subject
       are hereby renumbered accordingly:

   SECTION 6. Credit Data. — x x x
         SECTION 7. Outsourcing. — x x x
         SECTION 8. Rights of the data subjects. — x x x

**SECTION 3.** ***Transitory Provisions.*** All LCs, FCs, and other persons acting as such shall register all online applications used for loan processing activities with the NPC in accordance with the applicable Rules on Registration of Data Processing Systems and Notifications regarding Automated Decision-Making within fifteen (15) days after the effectivity of this Circular or within thirty (30) days from the availability of the NPC's registration system, whichever comes later.

All online applications which will be made publicly available after the effectivity of this Circular shall be registered with the Commission in accordance with Section 2 (C) of this Circular.

**SECTION 4.** ***Separability Clause.*** If any portion or provision of this Circular is declared null and void, or unconstitutional, the other provisions not affected thereby shall continue to be in force and effect.

**SECTION 5.** ***Repealing Clause.*** All other rules, regulations, and issuances contrary to or inconsistent with the provisions of this Circular are deemed repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 6.** ***Effectivity.*** This Circular shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or a newspaper of general circulation.

[^1]: Metadata as used in this Circular is understood to be any information that may define or describe contact lists.
[^2]: Securities and Exchange Commission, “Prohibition on Unfair Debt Collection Practices of Financing Companies (FC) and Lending Companies (LC),” SEC Memorandum Circular No. 18, series of 2019 [SEC MEMO. CIRC. 18, s. 2019], § 1 (19 August 2019): Unfair collection practices are as those which use or involve threats of use of violence or other criminal means to harm the physical person, reputation or property of any person, as well as those which use threats to take any action that cannot be legally taken.
[^3]: See: Securities and Exchange Commission, “Prohibition on Unfair Debt Collection Practices of Financing Companies (FC) and Lending Companies (LC),” SEC Memorandum Circular No. 18, series of 2019 [SEC MEMO. CIRC. 18, s. 2019], § 1 (19 August 2019).
