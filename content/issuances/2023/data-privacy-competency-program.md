## Issuance Text

**WHEREAS,** Republic Act No. 10173 or the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|Data Privacy Act]] (DPA) mandates the NPC to administer and implement its provisions while developing plans and policies that strengthen the protection of personal data in the Philippines.

**WHEREAS,** Section 9 of the [[laws/implementing-rules-and-regulations-of-the-data-privacy-act-of-2012|IRR]] requires the NPC to develop, promulgate, review, or amend rules and regulations for the effective implementation of the DPA and to inform and educate the public on data privacy rights and responsibilities.

**WHEREAS,** the NPC, through the NPC PHIL-DPO Program, launched the DPO Accountability, Compliance, and Ethics (DPO ACE) training and the Training the Trainers (T3) Program to capacitate Data Protection Officers and expand public data privacy education before they were discontinued as ad hoc initiatives.

**WHEREAS,** there is a need to institutionalize those training programs through a formal Circular that adds clarity, accessibility, and consistency to NPC data privacy education efforts while keeping them aligned with the latest developments in applying and interpreting the DPA.

**WHEREAS,** the NPC shall design the Data Privacy Competency Program (Program), which consists of courses on the fundamental and operational aspects of the DPA and related projects aimed at improving data privacy education, without functioning as a professional accreditation or replacing the qualifications of a data privacy professional.

**WHEREAS,** Executive Order No. 292, Series of 1987 and Republic Act No. 8293 authorize agencies to charge seminar fees and require prior approval or royalties when their work is used for profit.

**WHEREFORE,** the NPC issues this Circular prescribing the guidelines for the Program, including the Data Privacy Foundational Course.

**SECTION 1.** ***Scope and Purpose.*** Through the Program, the NPC shall develop uniform curricula, modules, and learning outcomes (the Curriculum) to train and capacitate the public. The NPC will license qualified Training Providers to use a prescribed Curriculum, and those providers shall design and conduct Training Courses based on the specific Curriculum covered by the License. This Circular applies to the Data Privacy Foundational Course and other courses developed under the Program, which cover fundamental concepts and principles of Philippine data privacy law.

**SECTION 2.** ***Definition of Terms.*** Terms used in the DPA and its IRR are adopted herein, and the following definitions apply:

A. *"Commission"* refers to the Privacy Commissioner and the two Deputy Privacy Commissioners.
B. *"Curriculum"* refers to the syllabus, modules, and other materials the NPC develops to set a minimum standard for the conduct of courses under the Program.
C. *"Enrollee"* refers to a person who avails themselves of a Training Course offered by a licensed Training Provider.
D. *"License"* refers to the non-exclusive, non-assignable, and non-sublicensable authority granted by the NPC to a Training Provider to use a specific prescribed Curriculum.
E. *"NPC"* refers to the National Privacy Commission created under the DPA.
F. *"Training Course"* refers to the course designed by a Training Provider pursuant to a specific prescribed Curriculum.
G. *"Training Provider"* refers to any natural or juridical person that is qualified under this Circular and licensed by the NPC to conduct a Training Course to an Enrollee based on a specific prescribed Curriculum.

**SECTION 3.** ***Nature of the License.*** The NPC shall issue a License to a qualified Training Provider to design a Training Course based on a prescribed Curriculum. In all cases, the License is non-exclusive, non-assignable, and non-sublicensable and is issued after a written Agreement that prescribes the terms and conditions of the Program.

**SECTION 4.** ***Term and Renewal of License.*** The License is valid for one year and may be renewed within thirty days prior to expiration if the Training Provider continues to comply with Section 5 and the Agreement. Upon termination of a License for any reason, a Training Provider may no longer conduct a Training Course, use the prescribed Curriculum, or represent itself as licensed by the NPC to provide data privacy courses.

**SECTION 5.** ***General Qualifications of a Training Provider.*** A Training Provider must be duly organized, validly existing, in good standing with the Department of Trade and Industry or Securities and Exchange Commission, and without pending civil, criminal, or administrative actions, investigations, suits, or convictions.

**SECTION 6.** ***Training Course.*** A Training Provider shall design a Training Course based on the prescribed Curriculum attached to a specific License. Before offering the course to the public, the Training Provider must submit the materials to the NPC for approval, which shall assess consistency with the applicable Curriculum and learning outcomes.

**SECTION 7.** ***Training Course Fee.*** A Training Provider may charge a fee, subject to the maximum amount stated in the Agreement and periodic adjustments by the NPC.

**SECTION 8.** ***Royalties.*** The NPC may collect royalties from Training Providers who use the prescribed Curriculum, and the use and management of the Curriculum are governed by the Agreement.

**SECTION 9.** ***Examination.*** For specific Training Courses, the NPC may administer an examination to determine an Enrollee’s competency. Passing the associated Training Course is a prerequisite to take the examination, which may also require a separate fee charged by the NPC; Training Providers shall not charge additional fees for that examination.

**SECTION 10.** ***Completion; Effects.*** Completion of a Training Course or passing the NPC examination measures competency but does not constitute a professional certification in Philippine data privacy law. No certification is necessary to act as a data privacy professional, including a Data Protection Officer or Compliance Officer for Privacy. Training Providers may issue Certificates of Attendance, Completion, or equivalents to successful Enrollees.

**SECTION 11.** ***Monitoring and Reporting.*** The NPC shall periodically monitor each Training Course to ensure alignment with the prescribed Curriculum, as provided in the Agreement.

**SECTION 12.** ***Consultative Body.*** The NPC may form a Consultative Body of five member-volunteers appointed by the Commission for two-year terms, based on the Chairperson’s endorsement, to assist in formulating Curricula and learning outcomes for specific courses. Members must be Filipino citizens of good moral character, with integrity, competence, and at least five years of relevant experience.

**SECTION 13.** ***Qualification under the DPO ACE Program.*** Qualifications from the DPO ACE training remain valid for one year from the effectivity of this Circular.

**SECTION 14.** ***Separability Clause.*** If any portion of this Circular is declared null and void or unconstitutional, the remaining provisions continue in force.

**SECTION 15.** ***Repealing Clause.*** All other rules, regulations, and issuances contrary to or inconsistent with this Circular are repealed or modified accordingly.

**SECTION 16.** ***Effectivity.*** This Circular takes effect fifteen days after publication in the Official Gazette or a newspaper of general circulation.
