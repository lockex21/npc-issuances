## Issuance Text

**Advisory No. 2020-03**

**DATE:** 23 October 2020

**SUBJECT:** Guidelines for Workplaces and Establishments Processing Personal Data for COVID-19 Response

**SECTION 1.** ***Rationale.*** This Advisory provides additional guidance to supplement Joint Memorandum Circular (JMC) No. 20-04-A, Series of 2020, issued by the Department of Trade and Industry (DTI) and the Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE). It covers workplaces and establishments that collect employee health declaration forms and client or visitor contact tracing forms and implement measures to manage asymptomatic and symptomatic employees in the workplace.[^1]

**SECTION 2.** ***Establishments as personal information controllers (PICs).*** Subject to any amendments to the JMC and the role of establishments in contact tracing, establishments implementing the JMC are considered PICs because they control the processing of personal and sensitive personal information of employees, clients or customers, and visitors. As PICs, they must comply with the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|Data Privacy Act of 2012]] ([[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|DPA]]), its [[laws/implementing-rules-and-regulations-of-the-data-privacy-act-of-2012|Implementing Rules and Regulations]] ([[laws/implementing-rules-and-regulations-of-the-data-privacy-act-of-2012|IRR]]), and other issuances of the National Privacy Commission (NPC), including those on data subject rights.

**SECTION 3.** ***Processing personal data for COVID-19 prevention and control.*** Processing personal data required by law or regulation to support the government's COVID-19 response is allowed under the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|DPA]]. Establishments shall observe transparency, legitimate purpose, and proportionality, implement reasonable and appropriate security measures at each stage of the data lifecycle, and uphold data subject rights.

**SECTION 4.** ***Ensuring fairness and lawfulness in processing.*** Establishments shall collect personal data only for COVID-19 prevention and control, including contact tracing and the management of asymptomatic and symptomatic employees in the workplace.

A. Establishments shall limit collection to the purposes required for COVID-19 prevention and control, based on existing government issuances, including the Employee Health Declaration Form, the Client/Visitor Contact Tracing Form, and the Customer Information and Health Checklist.[^2]

B. Employees, clients or customers, and visitors must be informed through a privacy notice that is easy to access and understand, using clear and plain language. The notice shall:

1. be visible within the premises or establishment where the data are collected, such as entry points and other conspicuous areas;
2. if personal data are collected through electronic means, be displayed so that a reasonable person can immediately notice and access it. When QR codes are used, the privacy notice should be posted beside the QR code with the contact number of the DPO; and
3. include:
   a. a description of the personal data to be entered into the system;
   b. the purpose or purposes for processing, limited to contact tracing and the management of asymptomatic and symptomatic employees in the workplace;
   c. the basis for processing, namely the DTI, DOLE, and Department of Health (DOH) guidelines requiring the accomplishment of the forms for employees, visitors, and clients;[^3]
   d. the scope and method of processing, including how the data will be used, stored, protected, and eventually disposed;
   e. the recipients to whom data may be disclosed, such as the DOH, its partner agencies, local government units (LGUs), or other authorized persons, and the reason for disclosure;
   f. the methods used for automated access by the recipient and the expected consequences to data subjects;
   g. the identity and contact details of the PIC, including the DPO contact information;
   h. the retention period, which shall be thirty (30) days for data collected through the employee health declaration form and client or visitor contact tracing form;[^4]
   i. the rights available to data subjects under the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|DPA]] and how they can exercise those rights through an appropriate channel or procedure within the establishment.
4. For further information on the processing activity, establishments may direct employees, clients or customers, and visitors to the official websites or social media pages of the establishment or pertinent government agencies.
5. If employees, customers, or visitors request an explanation, the establishment must be able to provide it through authorized personnel.

C. To ensure data quality and accuracy, security personnel or other authorized personnel shall:

1. ensure that submitted personal data are accurate and legible;
2. not undertake intrusive identity checks unless the same is part of a documented regular procedure; and
3. ensure that the date and time of shift or visit are correctly written.

D. Repurposing collected personal data for direct marketing, profiling, or any other commercial or non-commercial purpose is prohibited.

E. Establishments shall remind employees and third-party service providers, such as security personnel, that using collected personal data for any other purpose is punishable under the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|DPA]].

**SECTION 5.** ***General guidelines.*** The following safeguards shall be observed in the processing of personal data for COVID-19 response.

A. Collection through physical or electronic forms. Establishments may collect personal data through paper-based forms or electronically through digital or online forms. They shall:

1. provide a designated area where employees and clients or visitors can complete the forms while observing physical distancing;
2. when QR codes are used, provide unique QR codes for personnel or posted codes for visitors, and ensure that the linked electronic form is secure; and
3. require security personnel or other authorized personnel to safeguard the Employee Health Declaration and Client/Visitor Contact Tracing Forms and ensure that:
   a. paper-based systems are kept physically segregated, not openly accessible, and handled only by designated personnel;
   b. digital forms are encrypted, properly validated, and protected against intentional breach; and
   c. if public-use devices are provided for data entry, the operating system and security patches are kept up to date, browser autofill is disabled, devices are scanned for malware, and access is limited with automatic lock, password or biometric protection, and remote wipe when practical.

B. Collection through other means. Establishments may collect the data of employees, clients, customers, visitors, and close contacts through COVID-19 testing registration forms, quarantine reports, CCTVs, and other similar means. All forms and monitoring information required during quarantine shall be appropriately secured. Access to and use of personal data collected through CCTVs shall follow the guidelines and requirements of a separate NPC advisory when issued.

C. Use of the personal data collected. All establishments shall ensure that collected personal data are used only for contact-tracing measures and the management of asymptomatic and symptomatic employees in the workplace.

1. Policies and procedures shall be implemented to identify close contacts of a probable or confirmed COVID-19 case within the establishment while respecting the data privacy rights of employees, clients or customers, and visitors. In no case shall the identity of the suspect, probable, or confirmed case be disclosed to others when less privacy-intrusive means are available.
2. To address concerns about workplace safety and avoid disclosing the identity of any probable or confirmed case, the establishment, through the Human Resources or Safety Officer, shall pseudonymize data on probable or confirmed cases so that contact tracing and analysis may proceed without revealing identities.
3. The following documents and sources of information may be reviewed to determine close contacts:
   a. attendance records;
   b. CCTV footage;
   c. calendar of meetings;
   d. Client/Visitor Contact Tracing Form;
   e. Employee Daily Health Declaration Form; and
   f. logbooks in facilities of exposure, such as cafeterias, shuttle services, locker rooms, sleeping quarters, production floors, and comfort rooms.
4. In conducting interviews to determine close contacts, interviewees shall be informed of the purpose, scope, and extent of the processing of personal data involved in the contact tracing activity.
5. Employers are encouraged to collaborate with national or local government testing efforts, such as drive-thru or walk-thru testing facilities.[^5] If they do, employees must be informed of the recipient agencies, the purpose of sharing, the categories of personal data concerned, the existence of their rights and how they may exercise them, and other information sufficient to describe the nature and extent of the sharing.
6. Symptomatic employees should update their employer regarding COVID-19 test results from a nationally accredited testing facility. The employer must ensure that the data collected from those reports are properly collected, stored, and used only for contact tracing and for preventing and managing the spread of the disease in the workplace, including coordination with the nearest health care facility to refer the symptomatic employee under the existing DOH guidelines.[^6]
7. Employers shall inform the LGU or LGUs having jurisdiction over the workplace and the respective residence or residences of symptomatic employees and close contacts before testing for monitoring purposes.[^7] The employee's personal data must be securely shared or transferred and disclosed only to the proper authorities.

D. Storage and retention. Personal data collected shall be retained only for as long as necessary for the fulfillment of the purpose or purposes for which the personal data were obtained.

1. Authorized personnel shall ensure that physical forms, COVID testing registration forms, quarantine reports, and other pertinent documents are kept in a secure place at the end of business hours. Digital forms shall be secured using appropriate measures, including encryption.
2. Establishments shall implement access control policies that identify and limit the personnel authorized to access the personal data collected for the COVID-19 response. Authorized personnel shall be adequately instructed on the proper handling of these forms and other information, and may be required to execute a non-disclosure agreement.
3. Establishments shall be prepared to restore the availability and access to personal data in a timely manner in case of a physical or technical incident through the implementation of backup policies and procedures.
4. Safeguards to protect computer networks or physical records against accidental, unlawful, or unauthorized use or access shall be ensured by adopting the appropriate policies, such as network, information technology, information security, and bring your own device policies.
5. An Information Security Incident Management Policy containing procedures for regular monitoring, identification of foreseeable vulnerabilities, and preventive, corrective, and mitigating actions is recommended.
6. Personal data collected for contact tracing shall be retained only for the period allowed by existing government issuances and disposed of properly after thirty (30) days from accomplishment.[^8]
7. Other personal data collected for the management of probable, suspected, and confirmed COVID-19 patients shall be stored only for as long as necessary or while the purpose for processing still exists.

E. Disclosure or sharing. Disclosure of personal data collected through the health declaration form or other similar or related forms shall be limited to the DOH, its partner agencies, LGUs, or authorized entities, officers, or personnel, and only for the purpose of conducting contact tracing or managing probable, suspected, and confirmed COVID-19 patients.

1. In complying with reportorial requirements, all PICs shall ensure secure transmission and shall:
   a. keep records of all submissions and transmittals of reportorial requirements;
   b. comply with existing government regulations, including:
      1. reporting COVID-19 test results to the DOH in accordance with DOH Administrative Order No. 2020-0013;
      2. reporting COVID-19 positive employees, symptomatic employees, and their close contacts to the local health office and the Barangay Health Emergency Team (BHERT) of their place of residence, in accordance with DOH DM No. 2020-0189; and
      3. monthly reporting to the DOLE through its Regional Office, copy furnished to the DOH, of illness, diseases, and injuries in accordance with Section X of the DTI-DOLE Interim Guidelines on Workplace Prevention and Control of COVID-19 using the Work Accident/Illness Report (WAIR) COVID-19 form.
   c. have procedures in place to verify the genuineness of any request made by an alleged contact tracing team;
   d. prohibit disclosure of a patient's personal data to the public, the media, or any other public-facing platforms without the written consent of the patient or the patient's authorized representative or next of kin;[^9]
   e. ensure strict compliance with the protocols established by the DOH and LGUs for disclosing information through contact tracing of close contacts;[^10] and
   f. coordinate referrals of symptomatic individuals to the nearest health care facility under the latest DOH interim guidelines.[^11]

F. Disposal or destruction. Personal data shall be disposed of securely after the required retention period. Paper records must be shredded properly, while storage media and back-up data must be electronically wiped to ensure that stored personal data are beyond recovery. A Data Disposal Policy shall be implemented accordingly.

**SECTION 6.** ***Data subject rights.*** The rights and redresses under the [[laws/data-privacy-act-of-2012|DPA]] are available to employees, clients or customers, visitors, and their close contacts. Establishments and workplaces are bound to uphold data subject rights.

Any doubt in the interpretation of any provision of this Advisory shall be liberally interpreted in a manner mindful of the rights and interests of the data subject.
