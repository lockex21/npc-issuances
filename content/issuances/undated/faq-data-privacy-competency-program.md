## Issuance Text

**FAQs on the Data Privacy Competency Program**

Questions Raised During the Public Consultation on 16 June 2023

**I. DPO ACE and T3 Programs**

1. **Will the Data Privacy Competency Program replace the DPO ACE and T3 Programs?** Yes. The new Program replaces both initiatives, which were ad hoc and not supported by a formal NPC issuance. The NPC wants to formalize its engagement with Training Providers and address the gaps in the earlier education efforts. DPO ACE is not a prerequisite for courses under the new Program.

2. **Will passers of the DPO ACE Level 1 Examination need to train again?** No. They may still say they passed the DPO ACE Level 1 Examination. The new Program instead lets data privacy professionals take other courses that better fit their current stage.

3. **What is the significance of the one-year period in Section 13 of the Circular?** The DPO ACE Program began in 2018 and its first batch was taught in early 2019, so the concepts taught then may no longer reflect how the NPC currently interprets the DPA. The one-year period provides a transition.

4. **Is certification under the Data Privacy Competency Program valid for one year?** No. Completion of the Program or any of its courses does not produce certification. It may only result in a Competency Assessment, and passing that assessment is not a professional certification. The NPC is not a certifying body, and no professional certification is needed to perform data privacy work.

5. **What happens to existing accreditations under the T3 Program?** They will no longer have effect. Providers that want to continue may apply or enter into new agreements with the NPC, and training will now be done on a per-course basis.

**II. Nature Of License**

1. **What does "non-exclusive license" mean?** Training Providers cannot monopolize the curriculum or the courses. The Program is intended to democratize access to data privacy education and expand participation, especially outside Metro Manila.

2. **Will there be a different license for each curriculum developed by the NPC?** Yes. The NPC and the Training Providers will enter into a license or agreement on a per-course or per-curriculum basis.

3. **Will a curriculum under a specific license undergo periodic review?** Yes. The license is for one year so content can be updated and provider performance can be monitored. The agreement will be the same for all Training Providers so no one gets special terms.

4. **What are the penalties for Training Providers who violate the Circular and the agreement?** Their licenses may be revoked or suspended. The breaches that can trigger those remedies will be set out in the Circular and the formal written agreement.

5. **Will the license be renewed annually?** Yes. Annual renewal helps ensure that Training Providers are doing what they are supposed to do, with regular reporting and feedback during the year.

**III. Formal And Written Agreement**

1. **What will be included in the agreement between the NPC and a Training Provider?** It should include the obligations of both parties, timelines, qualifications of the Training Provider, and reportorial requirements. It should also cover the grounds for suspension or termination, along with royalties and fees.

2. **Is the agreement coterminous with the license granted to a Training Provider?** Yes. If the license is suspended, revoked, or terminated, the formal written agreement is affected the same way.

**IV. Consultative Body**

1. **Will there be a single Consultative Body for the Program?** No. There will be different Consultative Bodies for each course. The NPC wants practical input from practitioners and experts so the courses are operational and relevant.

2. **What are the responsibilities of the Consultative Body?** They vary by course, but generally the body gives the NPC an operational perspective and helps develop practical courses.

3. **Why is the term of members of the Consultative Body two years?** The term gives enough continuity to see a course through implementation while still being a reasonable voluntary commitment.

4. **How will the NPC ensure that Consultative Body members are competent and knowledgeable?** The Chairperson will nominate members, and the Commission En Banc will vet and appoint those best qualified.

5. **Who will be the Chairperson of the Program?** The Chairperson will always be one of the members of the NPC Commission En Banc.

6. **If Consultative Body work is pro bono, why may members receive compensation?** The NPC is not asking the body to create the curriculum. It wants expert advice so it can develop practical courses, and the compensation issue arises from that advisory role.

7. **Why should the NPC profit from collaborative work if members contribute pro bono?** The NPC will consult the body on specific aspects of a curriculum, but the body is not making the curriculum itself. The NPC believes the resulting education benefits everyone.

8. **Can the number of volunteers be increased, and what qualifications are required?** Five members is already a balanced number. Qualifications will differ per course, and setting them in the Circular itself may be too restrictive. The selection process will be transparent.

**V. Royalties**

1. **Why is the NPC collecting royalties?** The NPC wants a uniform curriculum that is easy to teach and understand while ensuring quality data privacy education. Royalties also help recoup some of the cost of operating the Program.

2. **Why not collect a one-time application fee instead?** A one-time fee may be too burdensome for smaller Training Providers. A royalty agreement lets them compensate the NPC from the fees they collect from students and supports broader participation.

**VI. Fees**

1. **How will the maximum training course fee be computed?** It will depend on the courses offered and will be set in the formal agreement for the course. The NPC wants to consult with Training Providers to arrive at a fair amount.

**VII. Qualification Requirements Of Training Providers And Trainers**

1. **Will the NPC require Training Providers and trainers to be experts in data privacy?** Ideally yes for the providers, but the NPC will not vet every trainer individually. It will assess providers through submissions, student feedback, pass rates, and similar indicators, while trusting providers to engage qualified trainers.

2. **Will the NPC specify other qualification requirements for Training Providers and trainers?** Yes, but those details will be in the specific agreement or contract for the course.

3. **May a licensed Individual Training Provider act as a trainer in a licensed Institutional Training Provider?** Yes, if the person applies individually.

4. **How will the NPC monitor and evaluate the performance of the Training Provider?** It will look at course quality, exam passers, student feedback, and periodic in-person observation based on submitted schedules.

**VIII. Training Course And Materials**

1. **Will the NPC require a specific training format?** No. Training Providers may choose their own format, but the NPC will provide the uniform curriculum as the basis for their course design.

2. **What falls under Training Course Materials subject to NPC approval?** Outlines, slide decks, handouts, lesson plans, and similar materials that form part of the Training Provider’s main lesson plan.

3. **Must a Training Provider seek approval whenever Training Course Materials change?** No. If the course still meets the minimum standards in the uniform curriculum, the Training Provider may simply submit a copy of the changes to the NPC.

**IX. Exam / Completion**

1. **Will the exam be conducted regularly for all courses?** No. Not all courses will have an exam at the end. For courses that do, the NPC expects exam schedules to be given in advance.

2. **If there is no exam for a particular course, how will competency be measured?** The Training Provider should ensure competency through the standardized curriculum and prescribed learning outcomes. Exams are not the only way to measure competency.

3. **Will Training Providers and the Consultative Body help prepare and design exams?** No. The NPC will do the exam design itself to avoid any appearance of partiality or bias.

4. **May a Training Provider conduct an NPC-prepared exam?** No. Only the NPC will conduct the exam it prepared.

5. **May a Training Provider administer its own exams?** Yes. The Circular does not prohibit that, and it also does not prohibit providers from teaching other data privacy courses outside the Program.

**X. Additional Questions During The Public Consultation**

1. **Is there a minimum enrollment requirement for renewal?** No. A minimum could limit access to data privacy education. The NPC wants the Program to remain accessible to people who may not do this full time.

2. **Will the NPC partner with IAPP to create something like CIPP/E?** Not in that form. The NPC is working with IAPP so the DPA will be included in any course they give under CIPP/A. A Philippines-specific CIPP may happen in the future if the market makes sense.

3. **Is this Program being practiced in other jurisdictions?** Not exactly, but similar regulator-led foundational programs exist, such as Singapore’s PDPC resources. The goal here is still to provide a practical entry point for data privacy education.

4. **Will privacy courses taught as part of degree programs be included?** Only if they offer the same course the NPC launches under the Program. The NPC is not trying to regulate all privacy education; it only covers courses launched under this Circular.

5. **Is the NPC considering a test run before final implementation?** Yes, that is under consideration, and the NPC may partner with interested Training Providers if it does.

6. **May Training Providers offer other services like consultancy or PIA assistance?** Yes. Those services do not conflict with teaching.

7. **How will the NPC determine the royalty rate and ensure transparency?** It will likely be a single amount rather than a range, discussed with Training Providers. The NPC may ask providers to submit the number of students trained and, where exams exist, correlate that with exam takers.

8. **What happens if a training institution does not follow the Program as designed by the NPC?** If it wants to partner with the NPC on this Program, it is expected to follow the Program. Failure to do so may result in revocation or suspension of the license granted to that institution.

9. **May former T3 partners still provide DPO ACE training?** They may still offer such courses, but they must clearly tell enrollees that DPO ACE has been discontinued and that no DPO ACE exam will be given.

10. **Will the NPC rank training institutions by pass rate?** The NPC will use pass-rate data to monitor performance and renewal decisions, but it does not intend to publish rankings.

11. **Other than a court order, can any government agency stop the Program?** No. The Program is a framework for data privacy education that the NPC can implement without issuing a new circular each time.

12. **Will this Program have a specific tenure?** No. As long as the NPC exists and there is a need to educate the public, the Program may continue.

13. **Can an individual take an NPC exam without enrolling in an accredited trainer?** No. The exam is tied to enrollment with a particular Training Provider.

14. **Is there already an exam fee amount?** Not yet. The NPC will consult with Training Providers before setting the fee.

15. **Will there be new certification levels for the courses?** No certifications will result from the courses. If there are levels, they will be in the sense that later courses build on earlier ones.

16. **Is a public-sector employee required to enroll with private trainers before acting as a privacy professional?** No. An individual is not required to enroll in any Training Provider before acting as a privacy professional.

[^1]: The NPC wants to emphasize that there is no professional certification necessary to carry out the functions of a data privacy professional in the Philippines.
